With the internet, knowledge has become a free commodity. Being able to Google the right thing, then finding the solution to your problem is a skill. I told my nephew that as long as you're able to go line by line and explain what happens, and you take ownership of that code, then you're good to go. You won't need to memorize anything to get started, the more you do, the more you can retain anyway.



The tutor is right. There are many core things you have to commit to. It's not about memory or the internet is easy to find, it's more about what all the knowledge adds up to when it's in your head.

Think of driving, can you drive when thinking about it? Can you copy and paste the ability to drive? If someone has to read a manual for driving every time they drive? What if an F1 driver didn't remember the steering wheel?

Copy and pasting a bit of code is not terrible when you think of it in isolation, we all forget from time to tim but are you a "code jockey" or a coder.

How can anyone be taken seriously if they don't know basic things in our coding world?

I think you have done the child a disservice by distracting him and the tutor. Also demeaning the tutor.
